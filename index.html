Problems I Have Solved

Amazon's interview process is built around real stories. Here are mine - in the exact format Amazon uses.

INVENTORY & COST CONTROL

Fresh stock was spoiling at 20-25% monthly - eating profit and frustrating customers.

SITUATION

I owned a retail grocery store stocking 200+ product lines, including fresh produce with short shelf lives. Spoilage was high and unpredictable.

TASK

Reduce waste without cutting product variety or raising prices for customers.

ACTION

Implemented a strict FIFO (First In, First Out) rotation system across all perishable lines. Introduced weekly stock audits every Monday, tracking sell-through rates per SKU. Renegotiated delivery frequency with 3 produce suppliers from weekly to twice-weekly to receive smaller, fresher batches.

RESULT

Spoilage dropped by approximately 20% within 6 weeks. Monthly waste costs fell by an estimated CAD $400-600. Zero customer complaints about product freshness in the following 8 months.

~CAD $500/mo saved

TEAM PERFORMANCE & ACCOUNTABILITY

A development team was missing sprint deadlines - 2 late deliverables per sprint with no accountability system.

SITUATION

I joined InnoBrain Technologies as an intern. Within weeks I was promoted to lead a team of 3 junior interns. The team had no standup culture and no blocker escalation process - deadlines were being missed every sprint.

TASK

Fix the delivery rhythm without damaging team morale as a first-time team lead aged 21.

ACTION

Introduced a 10-minute daily standup - each person stated what they finished, what they were doing next, and any blockers. Created a shared task board. Personally unblocked teammates within 2 hours when they were stuck rather than letting issues sit overnight.

RESULT

Late deliverables dropped from 2 per sprint to zero across the team's final 3 sprints. Both client applications were delivered on time and accepted into production.

0 missed deadlines

COACHING & INDIVIDUAL PERFORMANCE

15 students needed to pass a competitive government selection exam - failure would cost them a career opportunity.

SITUATION

While teaching at Bachpan NGO, I was asked to prepare 15 students for a high-stakes competitive government selection exam in addition to my regular classroom duties.

TASK

Maximise the number of students selected within a fixed preparation window, working with students of different ability levels.

ACTION

Assessed each student individually to identify their weak areas. Created personalised practice plans per student. Ran timed mock exams every 2 weeks, tracked scores, and adjusted the plan for each student based on their results. Gave targeted one-on-one sessions to the 4 students at highest risk of failing.

RESULT

14 out of 15 students were selected - a 93% pass rate on a competitive government exam.

93% pass rate
